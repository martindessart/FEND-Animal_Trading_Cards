Hemeroplanes Ornatus

This dude is not a snake but a real caterpillar that is doing mimicry when it feels in danger.

  • Scientific Name: Hemeroplanes ornatus
  • Average Wingspan: 90-95 mm
  • Localisation: Northern South America
  • Body Color: Black with yellowish bands and fringes

This guys also have a short silver mark. When the caterpillar H.ornatus is disturbed, it hangs from the plant and puffs up its head and creates two false eyes. Then, when someone try to attack, it can move back and forth like a snake and strikes at predators. We're lucky that it can't create real venom !
